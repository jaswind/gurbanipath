## Translations and transliterations

### English translation
**Bhai Sant Singh Khalsa, MD** — His complete English translation of Sri
Guru Granth Sahib Ji has been freely circulated for decades and is widely
used across the Sikh digital ecosystem. The translation is in the public
domain. We are deeply grateful for his decades of seva.

### Roman transliteration
**Dr. Kulbir Singh Thind** — His Roman transliteration of the entire
SGGS is the foundational digital resource that nearly every Sikh app and
website has been built on for the past 30+ years. He has freely shared
this work without restriction, asking only that it be used in service of
the panth. We use it with profound gratitude.

### Source compilation
The compiled source from which our text was extracted is widely
distributed as *"Sri Guru Granth Sahib Ji Darpan English"*. The original
compilers and digitizers of this resource are unknown to us; if you know
them, please tell us so we may credit them properly.

## Inspiration and prior art

We are grateful to the existing Sikh digital ecosystem that paved the
way and from which we have learned:

- **[BaniDB](https://banidb.com)** — the most thorough open SGGS database
  project, maintained by the Khalis Foundation; future versions of
  Gurbani Path may consume BaniDB upstream rather than re-deriving text
- **[SikhiToTheMax](https://sikhitothemax.org)** — the most widely-used
  Gurbani presentation app; it set many UX expectations the panth
  has come to rely on
- **[iGurbani](https://igurbani.com)** — beautiful mobile reading
  experience; influenced this project's typography
- **Sundar Gutka, KhalisFoundation, Sikhnet** — and the broader Sikh
  open-source community

This project is not a competitor to any of the above — there is room
for many tools, each with different strengths and approaches.
