# Code of Conduct

Gurbani Path is offered as seva. Anyone who uses, contributes to, or
reports issues for this project is part of a community gathered around
sacred work. We ask that this community uphold simple values:

## We expect

- **Respect** — for Sri Guru Granth Sahib Ji, for the panth, for fellow
  contributors, and for users from every background.
- **Patience** — maintainers are volunteers; users are sometimes
  frustrated; corrections take time.
- **Honesty** — about what works, what doesn't, what's accurate, and
  what's an error. Acknowledge mistakes openly when they happen.
- **Humility** — about the limits of what any tool can do, and about
  the imperfection of any human work in the presence of Bani.
- **Kindness** — particularly when correcting others, particularly
  when receiving criticism.

## We do not accept

- Disrespect toward the Guru, the panth, or any human being.
- Sectarian, political, or commercial use of this codebase or community.
- Personal attacks, harassment, or targeted unkindness in Issues, pull
  requests, or any other project space.
- Inserting advertising, tracking, or any form of user data collection
  beyond what is documented in `public/privacy.html`.

## Resolution

Most disagreements resolve themselves with goodwill and clarity. When
they don't, maintainers will:

1. Listen to all parties and read context carefully.
2. Address behavior, not identity.
3. Decide what is best for the project and the sangat using it.
4. Communicate the decision openly.

In rare cases where behavior continues to harm the project or its users,
contributors may have their commit access revoked or be asked to step
back from project spaces.

## Reach us

If you experience or witness conduct that troubles you, please contact
the maintainers privately at **feedback@gurbanipath.org** (alias to be
configured). All reports are taken seriously and handled discreetly.

---

*This Code of Conduct is intentionally short. It is not a legal
document; it is a reminder of how we hope to treat each other while
doing this work together. Adapted in spirit from the
[Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org), softened
for a sangat-led context.*
